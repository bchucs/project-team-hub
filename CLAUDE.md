- I want to build a web application similar to the Common App, but specifically for Cornell project teams. The platform will support roughly 30 project teams, each with distinct goals and missions. While teams are unique, they can be grouped into broader categories such as global health, automotive, flight, robotics, and similar domains. Students should be able to browse, search, and filter teams by category and interests, then add selected teams to a personal application dashboard. From there, applicants can complete team-specific essays and submit applications to each team. Each team defines its own essay prompts, and each essay has an enforced word or character limit that applicants must adhere to. The platform should clearly display these limits and prevent submissions that exceed them. Applicants upload a single resume that is shared across all applications. Essay prompts and requirements may vary by team, but submissions are handled centrally through the platform. The platform does not manage communication or decision-making. Individual teams will contact applicants directly outside of the system regarding interviews, decisions, or status updates. Each team should have a public profile page that includes basic information such as a description, category, website link, and social media links (e.g., Instagram).

Users will also have a general profile, which includes name, expected graduation year, major(s)/minor(s), netid, personal email, cornell email, phone number, and resume.
